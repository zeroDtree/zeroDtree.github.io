## Definition

A sequence $\{p_n\}$ in a metric space $X$ is said to converge if there is a point $p \in X$ with the following property: For every $\epsilon > 0$ there is an integer $N$ such that $n > N$ implies that $d(p_n, p) < \epsilon$. (Here $d$ denotes the distance in $X$.)
